## 1983 lease for 24 Queens Road (all text)

This LEASE is made the 12th day of December One thousand nine hundred and eighty-three BETWEEN DOROTHY BUTTERWORTH 24 Queens Road Hoylake Wirral Merseyside (hereinafter called 'the Lessor') of the one part and HERBERT HOLLAND and KATHLEEN AY HOLLAND (his wife) both of 24: Queens Road Hoylake Wirral aforesaid (hereinafter called "the Lessee") of the other part WHEREAS the Lessor has agreed with the Lessee in consideration of the rent hereinafter reserved and the covenants on the part of the Lessor and Lessee hereinafter contained to grant this Lease

NOW THIS DEED WITNESSETH as follows:-
1.IN consideration of the rent and the Lessees covenants hereinafter contained and reserved the Lessor hereby DEMISES unto
the Lessee ALL THAT the property described in the First Schedule hereto (hereinafter called "the Lower Flat") subject to
the rights contained in the Third Schedule hereto excepting and reserving out of this Lease for the benefit of the property described in the Second Schedule hereto (hereinafter called 'the Upper Flat') the rights set forth in the Fourth Schedule
hereto TO HOLD the Lower flat except and reserved as aforesaid unto the Lessee for a term of 999 years from the date hereof at the
rent of one peppercorn subject to the covenants conditions and stipulations hereinafter contained
2. THE Lessee to the intent and so as to bind the lower flat and so as to benefit and protect t~e upper flat and each and every part thereof hereby covenants with the Lessor and her successors in title that the Lessee and the persons deriving title under them will at all times hereafter observe and perform the stipulations and obligations set out in the fifth schedule hereto
3. THE Lessor to the intent and so as to bind the upper flat and to benefit and protect the Lower flat and each and every part thereof hereby covenants with tqe Lessee that the Lessor and the persons deriving title uncer her will at all times hereafter observe and perform the stipulations and obligations set out in the Sixth Schedule hereto
4. IT IS HEREBY CERTIFIED that the transaction hereby effected does not form part of a larger transaction or of a series of transactions in respect of which the amount or value or the aggregate amount or value of the consideration exceeds Twenty-five thousand pounds

IN WITNESS whereof the parties hereto have hereunto set their hands and seals the day and year first before written

3. THE FIFTH SCHEDULE
Stipulations and obligations to be observed
and performed the Lessee
1. Not to lessen or diminish the support or protection now given or afforded to all parts of the upper flat by the lower flat
2. Not at any time to make any structural alterations modifications or additions to the lower flat (including the front garden) without first obtaining the consent of the owner of the Lessor such consent not to be unreasonably withheld
3. Not to obstruct by vehicles deposited materials or otherwise the free passage of the Lessor and other persons entitled to rights of way over the vestibule edged green on plan number 1 and the driveway coloured
green on plan number 2 and not to park any vehicle caravan or house on wheels thereon
4. Not to cause or permit any obstruction of any drain or pipe used I in common with the Lessor for the passage of water or soil
5. Not to use the flat or allow or permit the same to be used for any purpose other than as a single private dwellinghouse otherwise than as 'the residence of a practitioner of medicine or surgery or registered
dental practitioner only and for no other purpose but so that such practitioners shall not receive lunatics or other patients to reside in the premises nor have any shop or dispensary for dispensing or selling drugs thereon nor for any purpose which may be or become a
nuisance or annoyance to theowner of the upper flat or any neighbouring owners or occupiers nor for any immoral or illegal purpose or whereby any insurance for the time being effected by the Lessor on the upper flat may be rendered void or voidable or whereby the rate of premium
may be increased
6. At all times hereafter to contribute and pay two fifths of all costs expenses and outgoings relating to the maintenance and repair of 
(i) the main structure including the main walls and timbers the roof chimney stacks and boundary walls of the dwellinghouse (but excluding rear garden walls)
(ii) the rain water drainage system serving the dwellinghouse including all gutters pipes and drains
(iii) the vestibule edged green on plan number 2 and the driveway coloured green on plan number 2
(iv) the gas and water pipes drains conduits and electrical wires and other gas water and electrical installations and the soil pipes serving the dwellinghouse and used or enjoyed by the Lessee and the
Lessor in common
(v) the cost of decorating (at least once in every five years) the exterior of the building in a colour to be mutually agreed between the owners for the time being of the upper flat and the lower flat and in default of agreement in the same colour as shall then be existing
7. At all times to insure and keep insured the Lower flat and all outbuildings against loss or damage by fire and the usual comprehensive risks in the joint names of the Lessor and the Lessee in a responsible Insurance Office of repute which shall be approved by the Lessor to the full replacement value thereof (the amount of the replacement value to be agreed between the Lessee and
the Lessor) and to make all payments necessary for the above mentioned purposes within fourteen days after the same shall respectively become payable and to produce to the Lessor not more often than once in every year on demand the policy or policies of such insurance and
the latest receipt for such payment 
8. To maintain the structure of the Lower flat in such a state of repair and condition as to ensure the maintenance of support and protection to the Upper flat so far as appropriate
9. Not to repair any joists or beams supporting the floors of the upper flat without giving notice to the Lessor of the intention to do so and giving full details of the work to be carried out and to be
5. At all times to ensure that the floor of the upper flat is carpeted with good and substantial carpets or other suitable materials sufficient to provide adequate and reasonable sound proofing between the Lower flat and Upper flat
6. Not to overload the floors of the Upper flat
7. In any lease of the Upper flat to include covenants by the Lessee in the same form (as near as circumstances permit) to those contained herein
