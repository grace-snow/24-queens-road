### Schedule of restrictive covenants
1. The following are details of the covenants contained in the Conveyance dated 11 July 1972 referred to in the Charges Register:- 
  "THE Purchaser hereby covenantss with the Vendor with the intent to bind (so far as practicable) the property hereby conveyed into whosesoever hands the same may come:-
  (a) Not to erect construct or place any additional building or erection upon the said property without first obtaining the consent in writing of the Vendor or its agent nor to make any structural or elevation alterational to any building or other structure now or hereafter erected constructed or placed upon the said property as aforesaid without first obtaining the like consent
  (b) Not to use the said premises or any part thereof otherwise than as a private residence or the residence of a practitioner of medicine or surgery or a registered dental practitioner only and for no other purpose but so that such practitioners shall not receive lunatics or other patients to reside in the premises nor have any shop or dispensary for dispensing or selling drugs thereon.
  (c) Not to do or cuase or suffer to be done or suffer upon the premises anything which shall or may be or become a nuisance (whether indictable or not) damage or annoyance to the Vendor or its successors in title or to the neighbourhood and not to fix fasten or place or permit or suffer to be affixed fastened or placed upon the premises or any part thereof any name plate (except the name of the house or a plate of the size and description commonly used by a medical man or a registered dentist) advertisement board placard or notice of any description."
2. The following are details of the covenants contained in the Conveyance dated 3 June 1975 referred to in the Charges Register:-
"THE Purchaser to the intent and so as to bind the Lower Flat and so as to benefit and protect the Upper Flat and each and every part thereof hereby covenants with the Vendor that the Purchaser and the persons deriving title under her will at all times hereafter observe and perform the stipulations and obligations set out in the Fifth Schedule hereto.

THE FIFTH SCHEDULE
Stipulations and obligations to be observed and performed by the Purchaser
1. Not to lessen or diminish the support or protection now given or afforded to all parts of the Upper Flat by the Lower Flat
2. Not at any time to make any structural alterations modifications or additions to the Lower Flat (including the front garden) without first obtaining the consent of the owner of the Upper Flat such consent not to be unreasonably withheld
3. Not to obstruct by vehicles deposited materials or otherwise the free passage of the Vendor and other persons entitled to rights of way over the vestibule edged green on Plan number one and the driveway coloured green on Plan number two and not to park any vehicle caravan or house-on-wheels thereon.
4. Not to cause or permit any obstruction of any drain or pipe used in common with the Vendor for the passage of water or soil.
5. Not to use the Flat or allow or permit the same to be used for any purpose other than as a single private dwellinghouse nor for any purpose which may be or become a nuisance or annoyance to the owner of the Upper Flat or any neighbouring owners or occupiers nor for any immoral or illegal purpose or whereby any insurance for the time being effected by the Vendor on the upper Flat may be rendered void or voidable or whereby the rate of premium may be increased.
6. At all times hereafter to contribute and pay two fifths of all costs expenses and outgoings relating to the maintaining and repairing of:-
  (i) the main structure including the main walls and timbers the  roof chimney-stacks and boundary walls of the dwellinghouse.
  (ii) the rain water drainage system serving the dwellinghouse including all gutters pipes and drains.
  (iii) the vestibule edged green on the said Plan number one and the driveway coloured green on the said Plan number two
  (iv) the gas and water pipes drains conduits and electrical wires and other gas water and electrical installations and the soil pipes serving the dwellinghouse and used or enjoyed by the Purchaser and the Vendor in common.
  (v) the cost of decorating (at least once in every five years) the exterior of the building in a colour to be mutually agreed between the owners for the time being of the Upper Flat and the Lower Flat and in default of agreement in the same colour as shall then be existing 
7. At all times to insure and keep insured the Lower Flat and all outbuildings against loss or damage by fire and the usual comprehensive risks in the joint names of the Vendor and the Purchaser with the Alliance Assurance Company Limited or some other insurance office of repute which shall be approved by the Vendor to the full replacement value thereof (the amount of the replacement value to be agreed between the Purchaser and the Vendor) and to make all payments necessary for the above mentioned purpose within fourteen days after the same shall respectively become payable and to produce to the Vendor not more often than one in every year on demand the policy or policies of such insurance and the latest receipt for such payment.
8. To maintain the structure of the Lower Flat in such a state of repair and conditiond as to ensure the maintenance of support and protection to the Upper Flat so far as appropriate.
9. Not to repair any joists or beams supporting the floors of the Upper Flat without giving notice to the Vendor of the intention to do so and giving full details of the work to be carried out and too be responsible for and to pay all costs in connection with repairing any damage caused to the floors of the Upper Flat arising out of repairs to the joists and floor of the Lower Flat.
10. To permit the Vendor and her Agents with or without workmen and others once a year at reasonable times and upon giving reasonable notice of an intention to do so to enter upon and examine the condition of the Lower Flat in order to ensure the due observance of the covenants herein contained and thereupon the Vendor may serve upon the Purchaser notice in writing specifying any repairs required calling upon the Purchaser forthwith to execute the same and if the Purchaser shall not within one month after service of such notice proceed diligently with the execution of such repairs then to permit the Vendor to enter upon the Lower Flat and execute such repairs the cost thereof to be a debt due to the Vendor from the Purchaser recoverable by action.
11. As often as the whole or any part of the Upper Flat shall be destroyed or damaged by fire explosion or aircraft escape of water or other like cause to rebuild and reinstate the Lower Flat in all material respects to its present condition and it is hereby agreed that any monies received in respect of the insurance of the Lower Flat herein covenanted to be paid by the Purchaser shall be applied so far as the same shall extent in so rebuilding or reinstating the Lower Flat
12. To keep the front garden and all hedges surrounding it in good order and condition.
13. Not to use any sound producing instruments or equipment in the Lower Flat between the hours of 11.00 pm and 9.00 am so as to be audible outside the Flat and at other times no such instrument or equipment shall be used so as to be a nuisance or annoyance to the occupiers of the Upper Flat
14. Not to exhibit any sign plate or placard in or upon the Lower Flat so as to be visible from the exterior thereof save a nameplate stating only the name of the occupier of the Lower Flat and the name or number thereof.
15. To keep in repair the pipes wires and mains exclusively serving the Lower Flat and not to permit the escape of gas from any such pipes.
16. To pay all assessments outgoings and all other charges so far as they are assessed separately on the Lower Flat."
